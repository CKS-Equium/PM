## colonygame / Hiraeth (2026-06-08) — DEV_PLAN_2 player-first QoL (playtest-gated)

- **When the gate is human playtest, PIVOT from adversarial code-review to a GUI-pitfall pre-playtest review** — *Situation:* the player-first QoL phase where the gate shifted from adversarial code-review to a **human playtest** (game-feel), so my usual review role was **largely not exercised** — I thinned out for most of the phase. *What happened:* meanwhile the operator caught, at the cost of a round-trip each, a whole bug class that is **static and diff-readable**: the build menu **rebuilt every Button every frame** (clicks lost — reported **twice**); the Recruit button's **enable-state didn't mirror the sim's satisfaction precondition** (a silently-no-op'ing "lying" button); **input + movement buried inside the fixed-timestep sim loop** (dropped clicks + choppy movement, no render interpolation); **FPS regressions** from always-on-top `NoDepthTest` labels + per-frame allocations; and **dev/sandbox affordances leaking into the player build**. I had spare capacity exactly when a different flavour of review would have paid off most (build & grow flow took **3 playtest rounds**). *Lesson:* in player-facing phases where the gate is human playtest, **pivot** — read the **GUI diff** for the known un-headless-verifiable classes **before** the orchestrator raises the `needs-human` playtest issue: (1) interactive controls rebuilt every frame; (2) UI enable-state not mirroring the sim's exact preconditions; (3) input/movement coupled to a fixed-timestep loop without interpolation; (4) per-frame allocations / always-on-top draws at scale; (5) dev/sandbox affordances in the player build. The adversarial eye still earns its keep — just aimed at the interaction layer the test net can't see, to spend operator round-trips on *fun* rather than diff-visible bugs. **Candidate process / contract change.** *(confidence: high)*

## colonygame / Hiraeth (2026-06-08) — execution-mode autonomous build

- **Stay adversarial on the gap between "green DoD" and "the product actually works" — especially on unattended runs — RECURRING positive (3rd project)** — *Situation:* an overnight autonomous run building a Godot/C# colony-sim, six gates, no human watching each step; each gate arrived with a green DoD (full build + passing unit tests + a passing headless `[GATE tN PASS]` scenario). *What happened:* I BLOCKed **T1** (smelter never smelted — economy didn't close; root: building kind inferred from an overloaded `reach` field, unguarded authored data) and **T4** (warehouses were a one-way "roach motel" — couriers deposited goods nothing read back, silently draining production), and flagged the **T2** distinct-units MAJOR test-gap — all three were "passes the gate, breaks the game" defects the green DoD + passing scenarios **hid**. Both BLOCKs were fixed before merge with regression tests of **proven teeth** (T1: flip the kind → red; T4: 15 stranded without the fix → 0 with). Net: **0 escaped defects to `main`**. This is the third consecutive project (team-pulse, hearthflix, colonygame) the review gate caught release-relevant bugs tests missed. *Lesson:* a green build + passing acceptance scenario does **not** mean the product works — hunt specifically for (1) **state that is produced/stored but never read/consumed** (warehouse stranding), (2) behaviour **inferred from incidental/overloaded fields** instead of explicit discriminators (the `reach` bug), and (3) tests/scenarios that assert **conservation/presence rather than end-use**, or that pass at a **degenerate value**. The review gate is **most** load-bearing on unattended/autonomous runs where no human is the safety net, and **BLOCK = fix-before-merge (not a human stop)** is the right shape — it kept quality high without ever stopping the run. *(confidence: high)*
